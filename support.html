Scam support

If you have lost money or shared your personal details with a scammer, taking immediate action is crucial to limit the damage and protect yourself from further loss.

Here are some essential steps to follow:

1. Contact your financial institution

• If you have made a payment or have shared your card details, inform your bank or card provider immediately. Ask them to stop any transactions or to cancel your card.

• If you're unsure whether you're being scammed, stop sending money right away and contact IDCARE for tailored advice.

2. Get further help to recover

• Contact IDCARE, a free national identity and cyber support service. They can help you step-by-step in limiting the damage.

- Call IDCARE on 1800 595 160, Monday–Friday: 8am–5pm AEST.

- Visit their website to learn more and get help online.

3. Report the scam

• Once you have secured your details, you can help others by reporting the scam to Scamwatch.

• Make an official online report to the police.

• If the scam occurred on a specific platform (like a social media site or online marketplace), report the scam to the platform so they can take action against the scammer.

• You can also call the Scams and Identity Theft Helpdesk at Services Australia, to report identity theft.

- Call the Scams and Identity Theft Helpdesk on 1800 941 126, Monday–Friday: 8am–5pm AEST.

4. Protect yourself moving forward

• If the scam involved the sharing of your personal details, change your passwords immediately to secure your online accounts.

• Continue to monitor your financial accounts closely for any suspicious activity and report anything unusual to your bank immediately.

• Scammers may attempt to target you again. Look out for new scams, especially if you do not know the person and you didn’t contact them first.

5. Get support

• If being scammed has caused you to become overwhelmed with debt, contact a financial counsellor as soon as possible. The earlier you get help, the better your options will be. This is a free and confidential service to help you get your debt under control.

• Being scammed can be an overwhelming and scary experience. If you need someone to talk to, contact Lifeline or Beyond Blue for emotional support.
